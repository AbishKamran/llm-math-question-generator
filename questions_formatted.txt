@question Each student choosing from the school cafeteria menu selects 1 sandwich and 1 drink. The table shows the options available. How many different lunch combinations are possible?

| Sandwich | Drink |
|:---:|:---:|
| Turkey | Water |
| Ham | Juice |
| Veggie | Milk |
| Chicken |  |

@instruction Choose the best answer from the options below.
@difficulty hard
@Order 1
@option 4
@option 7
@option 9
@@option 12
@option 17
@explanation
Using the multiplication principle: 4 sandwich options × 3 drink options = 12 total combinations.

**Real-world application:** This applies to menu planning in restaurants and cafeterias.

**Formula:** For independent choices, total combinations = n₁ × n₂
